ECON201 Lecture 1

topics: introduction to economics, utility, opportunity cost, economic systems

Economics is really about studying individual's decision under scarcity.

To consume, an individual seek to fulfill their utility/satisfaction. An important concept that relates to gaining utility is goods and services.

Apples, clothes, medicine, and so many things are familiar to you as a consumer/producer in the real world. They are the goods and services that you consume for exchange of utility.

To producers, all goods and services comes with opportunity cost. Suppose you love eating Japanese sushi, and you are also very fond of hotpot. When you plan to eat Japanese sushi for the night, the next best option you may choose is hotpot. In other words, when you decide to eat Japanese sushi, you are also giving up the opportunity to eat hotpot. You don't get the utility you'd gain from eating hotpot, if you end up eating sushi. The utility of eating hotpot, in this scenario, is the opportunity cost of you eating sushi.

Producers and consumers, goods and services altogether composes a large and somewhat complex system--an economy. A system where many entities produces and sells, and purchases goods and services. These entities are who we would call today, firms and markets.

An economic system may be identified as one of the three:

Traditional Economy, Command Economy, and Market Economy.

A traditional economy describes a system of which entities produce and consume their own products (ex: farmers grow their own vegetables and they are the only ones who consume their crops)

A command economy describes a system of which high authorities controls production and prices of products.

A market economy describes a system of which production and consumption are decided by individiual entities.
