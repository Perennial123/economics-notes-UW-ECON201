ECON201 Lecture 1

Economics is really about studying individual's decision making considering the scarcity of resources in the world.

To consume, an individual seek to fulfill their utility, or simply, satisfaction or happiness. An important concept that relates to gaining utility is goods and services.

Apples, clothes, medicine, and so many things are familiar to you as a consumer/producer in the real world. They are the goods and services that you consume for exchange of utility. A good and service can provide utility to consumers, and they are also products produced by producers.

To producers, all goods and services comes with opportunity cost. Suppose you love eating Japanese sushi, and you are also very fond of hotpot. When you plan to eat Japanese sushi for the night, the next best option you may choose is hotpot. In other words, when you decide to eat Japanese sushi, you are also giving up the opportunity to eat hotpot. You don't get the utility you'd gain from eating hotpot, if you end up eating sushi. The utility of eating hotpot, in this scenario, is the opportunity cost of you eating sushi.
